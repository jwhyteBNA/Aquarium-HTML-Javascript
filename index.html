Tank Tips

There are a few important aspects to consider when setting up your aquarium tank so that your fish live long, healthy lives. Here are some of the elements to pay attention to as you clean your fishes' home:

  • Salinity: The salinity, or salt content, in your water is determined by the type of fish you have.Always make sure the water has the right amount of salt to mimic your fishes' natural habitat. For example, saltwater fish need 35PPT, or 35 parts salt to every 1,000 parts water. You can measure this with a refractometer.
  • Temperature: Setting your tank to the right temperature is critical so your fish don't get shocked, leading to illness or fatality. Where you put the tank in a room can lead to opportunities for drafts from windows,doors, and heating vents. Aquarium lights can also impact your water temperature. Tropical fish typically need heated water between 75-80 degrees Fahrenheit (24-27 degrees Celcius). Invest in a dependable water heater and thermometer.
  • Cleaning: How often you clean your tank will depend on many facotrs, such as the tank size, how many fish you have, and how many live plants you have in the tank. You will leave the fish in the tank while you clean. You will complete basci steps such as removing bacteria and dirty water, maintaining tank plants and decor, clean the filter, and condition and test clean water before refilling. Other sites provide detailed cleaning instructions based on your needs.

Sites of Harvest

Travel Tips

The art of fish harvesting will lead you on adventures to some of the most incredible places on earth. Make the most of your trip by learning from some of my experiences:

  • Research. Before you ever leave your home, learn about your destination and any environmental regulations they may have. Follow local customs (and laws)!
  • Pack Smart. Based on your research, pack appropriate swimgear, tools, and other comfort items. Pro Tip: Backpacks are easier to haul than rolling luggage in remote areas.
  • Live Big. Enjoy all the local culture has to offer while harvesting your fish. Eat the cuisine, see where the citizens live and play, and learn about local culture.
  • Document. Take back memories too - bring journals to write, phone accessories to take photos, and keep your phone handy to record music and compile playlists.
